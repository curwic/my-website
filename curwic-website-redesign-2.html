Why Curwic exists
Ability is rarely the gap.
Systems and execution are.

Businesses underperform because their workflows and decision structures haven't kept up with their ambitions. Individuals underperform because they're missing a specific skill or system, not effort. Curwic addresses both, but as two separate practices: management consulting for organisations, and 1:1 coaching for individuals — each diagnostic-led, neither a repackaged version of the other.

— Founder's note: I built Curwic because I kept seeing the same pattern on both sides — capable teams and capable people, held back by a lack of system rather than a lack of ability. We're a founding cohort, which means direct access to the team building this, and both practices are still shaped by real client feedback.

Methodology

The diagnostic before the system.

Whether you're a business applying for Consulting or an individual applying for Coaching, we don't start with a template. We start by finding out what's actually limiting your performance — then build the system around that.

01

We diagnose before we prescribe

Every engagement starts with an evaluation call, not a fixed program. In Consulting, we're listening for where a business's decisions stall, workflows break down, or execution gets muddy under pressure. In Coaching, we're listening for the specific skill, habit, or system gap that's capping an individual's growth.

Same diagnostic principle, applied to two different subjects — an organisation's operations, or a person's career.

1:1 evaluation call Operational patterns No fixed template
02

We build a system specific to the practice, not a shared template

Once we know what's actually holding back performance, we build a system specific to it. Two clients who both say "we want better execution" might get entirely different systems — one because a business's handoffs between teams break down, another because an individual's priorities aren't structured.

In Consulting, this means an embedded partnership with your organisation from day one. In Coaching, it means a personalised 1:1 program built around your specific goals.

Consulting: embedded partnership Coaching: personalised 1:1 program
03

We work against your real operations, not case studies

Sessions are grounded in what's actually in front of you — the decision you're about to make, the workflow that's slowing your team down, the plan you need for next quarter. We'd rather work on the real thing than a hypothetical.

Applied implementation Real operations
04

We give direct feedback, on a cadence

Better performance needs honest feedback delivered consistently, not once a quarter. Every session ends with something concrete to implement before the next one, and we track whether it's actually moving results — not just whether it felt productive in the room.

Direct feedback Ongoing sessions
Our underlying belief

It's rarely a skills problem.
It's almost always a systems problem.

Most businesses and individuals who come to us already have the underlying ability. What they're missing is a structure that turns that ability into consistent execution — whether that's a business's workflow or an individual's skill set. We build a system that treats the specific bottleneck, instead of a generic program that treats everyone the same.

The problem

Panel cutting plans, made by hand.

Most puff/sandwich panel manufacturers still plan cutting layouts and production sequencing manually — on spreadsheets or on paper. It's slow, it's error-prone, and it quietly wastes raw material on every single run.

Panel-wastage optimisation

Feeds your order sizes into a nesting engine that finds the cutting layout with the least offcut, run after run.

Automatic run sequencing

Orders are grouped and sequenced to minimise machine changeovers and idle time on the line.

Live capacity view

See what's committed, what's free, and what today's plan actually looks like — without opening five spreadsheets.

Exportable cutting lists

One-click export to a printable or machine-ready cutting list, so the floor gets exactly what planning intended.

Details

Everything you'd ask us anyway.

Manufacturers and fabricators of puff/sandwich panels who currently plan cutting layouts and production runs manually and want to cut material waste and planning time.

Savings depend on your current process and order mix, but most teams moving off manual planning see a meaningful drop in offcut within the first few production runs.

PuffPPO accepts order data via CSV or a simple API, and exports cutting lists in common formats your floor already uses. No ERP replacement required.

Most teams are running their first optimised plan within a week, including a short onboarding call to map your panel sizes and machine constraints.
